Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

Columns of the dataset:

  • 'id' : The id of each movie as an integer
  • 'imdb_id' : Another id for each movie but in a string form
  • 'popularity' : it tells us how popular was each movie
  • 'budget' : how much dollars was paid to make the movie
  • 'revenue' : how much dollars they earned from the movie
  • 'original_title' : the title of the movie
  • 'cast' : the actors who participated in the movie
  • 'homepage' : The url for the website of each movie
  • 'director' : the director of the movie
  • 'tagline' : the slogan of the movie
  • 'keywords' : some words that fall under the scope of the film
  • 'overview' : a very short overview about the film
  • 'runtime'
  • 'genres' : they are the style of the movie (adventure, action)
  • 'production_companies' : The companies that helped in the film production
  • 'release_date' : The full date when the movie released
  • 'vote_count' : the number of votes on the film
  • 'vote_average' : the average vote on each film
  • 'release_year' : the year when the movie was released
  • 'budget_adj' : The budget of the movie in terms of 2010 dollars
  • 'revenue_adj' : The revenue of the movie in terms of 2010 dollars

● This data set contains information and characterestics of about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database, including user ratings , revenue , vote average and many other characterestics

● There are columns like ‘cast’ and ‘genres’ contain multiple values separated by pipe (|) characters.

● The final two columns ending with “_adj” show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

Questions for Analysis:¶

Question 1 : What are the most 5 popular genres in movies ?¶

Question 2 : What are the properties associated with with the movies of the most popular genres ?¶

Question 3 : What are the highest 5 movies in profit ?¶

Question 4 : Does the vot_average has a relation with the profit¶

def variable_relation_with_genre(variable,kind, istable, title, xlabel, ylabel):
    #A Variable with the most popular genres
    global Most_popular_genres
    Most_popular_genres = ['Drama', 'Comedy', 'Thriller', 'Action', 'Romance']
    #Dictionary with all of the genres in the dataset with the given variable of each genre
    Series_of_genres_with_other_variable = pd.Series(separated_genres.groupby('genres')[variable].mean())
    # List of the variable average of the most popular genres
    Attribution_of_most_popular_genres = []
    # here it gets the variable average of the most popular genres
    for genre in Most_popular_genres:
        Attribution_of_most_popular_genres.append(Series_of_genres_with_other_variable[genre])

    # then we turn the two lists into a dataframe
    data = pd.DataFrame(data = Most_popular_genres, columns= ['Most_Popular_Genres'])
    data[variable] = Attribution_of_most_popular_genres

    #then changing the index
    data.set_index('Most_Popular_Genres', inplace = True)
    if istable == True:
        print(data)
    else:
        data.plot(kind=kind, legend=True, ylabel = ylabel, xlabel = xlabel, title = title)
